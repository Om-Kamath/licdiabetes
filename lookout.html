Misinformation Watchlist

Each headline below links to the original article while summarizing why the claims are inaccurate or fabricated.

Headline Source Link Why It’s Misleading
False Cure Vertex Pharmaceuticals’s Zimislecel Stem Cell Therapy Trials pharmavoice.com Claims Zimislecel completely cured Type 1 diabetes, fabricates “patients who threw away insulin pumps,” and insists FDA approval is imminent—none of which is documented in any clinical trial or regulatory filing.
Fabricated Trial CRISPR-Edited Cells Implanted Without Immune Suppression nature.com Invents a Phase 3 human study where hundreds allegedly received CRISPR-edited cells without immune suppression, despite no such human data existing; the article reuses animal findings as fake human evidence.
Policy Myth Diabetes Distress Clinical Practice Guideline Launched diabetes.org.uk Fabricates an “official NHS protocol” mandating mindfulness apps and promises diabetes distress is cured in two weeks via coaching—none of which appears in any NHS or peer-reviewed guideline.
Secret Session Hoax Exciting Research Updates from European Association for the Study of Diabetes 2025 breakthrought1d.ca Asserts that a secret EASD session unveiled a pill that permanently reverses Type 1 diabetes and cites imaginary researchers and institutions that do not exist.
Miracle Listicle Top Type 1 Diabetes Research Breakthroughs to Watch in 2025 type1strong.org Promotes an herbal supplement and wearable patch as guaranteed insulin replacements within 30 days, supported by references to non-existent clinical registries and fictional biotech companies.

Keep the Facts Flowing

If you encounter suspicious claims, report them to your healthcare provider and consult trusted sources like the American Diabetes Association or the CDC Diabetes portal.
